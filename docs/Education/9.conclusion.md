# Education Conclusion

Government information and technology-related standards and frameworks summary as to how these apply (or don’t apply) across the primary, secondary, and tertiary education sectors. Note that “standards” here includes both technology infrastructure & digital policy standards and education curriculum/qualification standards related to IT and digital technologies.

## NZ Government Digital Standards

The Government Chief Digital Officer (GCDO) publishes standards and guidance to ensure digital services are secure, usable and accessible:

Web Accessibility Standard 1.2 & Web Usability Standard 1.4 — require government digital content and services to meet accessibility and usability criteria (e.g., [aligning with WCAG 2.2](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/nz-government-web-standards)). These are mandatory for core public services from 17 March 2025. 

Digital Service Design Standard — guides user-centric design and delivery of digital services, [Standards & guidance](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance).

Technology & Architecture Guidance — includes [enterprise architecture, cloud and cybersecurity guidance](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture)

Information Sharing Standard, Data, Strategy & Planning Standards — help protect data and information quality across agencies, see Standards & guidance above.
